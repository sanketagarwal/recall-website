An open research lab for AI-native finance

We build AI systems that operate in live financial markets for crypto, DeFi, traditional exchanges, and prediction markets. Everything we learn gets published.

Memo

Vision

A growing share of trading volume is already algorithmic. Autonomous agents are arbitraging across fragmented crypto venues in real time, LLMs are being wired into trading pipelines and the infrastructure required to operate at this intersection is evolving weekly.

And yet, the research is almost entirely closed. Quant funds study these dynamics but publish nothing. AI labs advance the models but don't test them where money is on the line.

We exist to conduct and execute on research operating at the intersection of artificial intelligence and financial markets. We build systems, deploy them into live environments, study how they perform, and publish our results.

We chose financial markets because they offer something no benchmark can replicate: adaptive adversaries, non-stationary data, nanosecond feedback loops, and genuine stakes. When an agent fails here, the lesson is real. When it succeeds, the insight is durable.

We believe the most important research at this intersection should be open, not proprietary.

Focus

Areas of Research

01

Agent Engineering

How to build, deploy, and keep alive autonomous AI systems operating in live financial markets. Architecture, orchestration, failure recovery, and the infrastructure that makes it possible.

  • Multi-venue execution and routing
  • Agent harness design for trading
  • Orchestrating 100+ agents in production
  • Failure modes and recovery patterns
02

AI Performance in Markets

Empirical research on how AI systems actually perform when given real capital and real markets. Arbitrage execution, directional trading, market-making which is tested, measured, published.

  • Can AI agents reliably execute arbitrage?
  • How do autonomous systems behave during volatility?
  • AI vs. human traders: what the data says
  • Should you trust AI with your money?
03

Market Structure & Intelligence

How AI is changing the structure of financial markets themselves. Volume analysis, liquidity dynamics, price discovery, and the systemic effects of autonomous participants.

  • What share of trading volume is now AI?
  • How do AI agents affect price discovery?
  • The role of AI in high-frequency trading
  • Fragmentation and liquidity in crypto markets
04

Open Tools & Infrastructure

Production-grade, open-source tooling extracted from our own systems. Execution routers, data pipelines, agent frameworks, and utilities for anyone building at the intersection of AI and markets.

  • Multi-venue execution engines
  • Real-time data ingestion pipelines
  • Agent evaluation and backtesting frameworks
  • Datasets for AI × markets research
